# Urban Mobility vs Economic Indicators in Latin America

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between urban mobility and economic performance across major Latin American cities during 2024.

The study integrates traffic congestion metrics from TomTom Traffic Index with economic indicators from OECD Cities to explore whether higher economic development is associated with better mobility outcomes.

---

## Business Question

Does a higher GDP per capita lead to lower traffic congestion in Latin American cities?

---

## Key Findings

### Mexico City

- Highest traffic delay: 2,833 hours
- Highest congestion length: 389 km
- Largest traffic event count: 595

### Bogotá

- Highest Traffic Index: 37.6
- Traffic delay: 1,142 hours

### Montevideo

- Highest GDP per capita: USD 26,176
- Lowest congestion levels

### Main Insight

Higher GDP per capita alone does not guarantee lower congestion levels.

Urban mobility appears to depend on additional factors such as transportation infrastructure, urban planning and population density.
